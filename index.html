About

Who We Are

UTHR is the University of Toronto Humanoid Robotics team. We are a multidisciplinary team of engineers, computer scientists, and designers building Canada's first full-scale bipedal humanoid robot from the ground up.

Founded in 2026, we work at the intersection of mechanical design, embedded systems, and machine learning. We are open to students across all disciplines and experience levels.

Divisions

Our Subteams

01 — Mechanical

Mechanical Engineering

Structural design, actuator integration, and the full physical architecture of the humanoid platform. From skeletal geometry to surface finish and manufacturing.

SolidWorks FEA Actuators Fabrication GD&T
02 — Electrical

Electrical Engineering

Power architecture, PCB design, motor drivers, and the embedded hardware backbone that brings the robot to life and keeps it operating reliably.

PCB Design Power Systems Motor Drivers Sensing
03 — Software

Software Engineering

ROS 2, perception pipelines, and the high-level autonomy stack running on onboard NVIDIA Jetson compute. Building the brain of the robot.

ROS 2 Perception Deep Learning C++ Python
04 — Controls

Controls Engineering

Whole-body dynamics, balance, gait generation, and the low-level control loops that make stable bipedal locomotion possible in real hardware.

MPC Gait Planning State Estimation Sim-to-Real
05 — Business & Sponsorship

Business & Sponsorship

Sponsorship acquisition, financial operations, industry partnerships, and the strategic relationships that fund the team's hardware and operations.

Sponsorship Finance Partnerships Strategy
06 — Research

Research

Applied research in locomotion, manipulation, and physical intelligence. Connecting the team's work to the broader academic and industry robotics community.

Locomotion Manipulation Publications ML
